What is Ascend?

Ascend is a Data Structures and Algorithms sheet curated by me.

The idea behind Ascend was never to create just another DSA sheet.
It was built after seeing countless students struggle with problem solving,
many of whom give up early because they look at a solution and think:

How would anyone ever come up with something like this?

The truth is that most people are not lacking intelligence - they simply lack exposure. Problem solving in DSA is largely built on familiarity. If you have never encountered a pattern before, it's difficult to recognize it, visualize it,
or apply it when needed.

Ascend is designed to bridge that gap.

Every problem in this sheet has been carefully selected and organized to help you gradually build intuition, recognize recurring patterns, and develop the ability to approach unfamiliar problems with confidence. The goal is not to memorize solutions, but to understand the thinking behind them.

Data Structures and Algorithms are an essential part of Computer Science. From search engines and social media platforms to operating systems and databases, efficient software relies on strong algorithmic foundations. Beyond interviews and coding assessments, learning DSA develops structured thinking, analytical reasoning, and a deeper understanding of how software works under the hood.

Ascend is my attempt to provide a clear, guided path through that journey - one that helps you move from confusion to confidence, one problem at a time.

Ascend isn't about solving more problems. It's about becoming the kind of person who can solve them.

Why Choose Ascend?

Mastering DSA is not about solving the most problems - it's about solving the right problems in the right order.

Structured & Focused

With nearly 400 carefully selected problems, Ascend is organized by algorithms, data structures, and their underlying patterns. By focusing on one pattern at a time, you build intuition and problem-solving skills instead of simply collecting solved questions.

Learn Patterns, Not Answers

Most DSA questions are built upon recurring ideas. Ascend groups related problems together to help you recognize patterns, not memorize answers. Over time, those patterns become instinctive when tackling new problems.

Why LeetCode?

Every problem comes from LeetCode. While platforms like Codeforces and CodeChef are excellent, LeetCode provides a cleaner experience and industry-relevant questions to build a strong foundation.

Track Your Ascension

Monitor your progress, track completed problems, and watch your consistency grow over time. Every solved question is a tangible step forward.

Who Am I?

Hey, I'm Aditya Talreja.

I'm a DSA and Competitive Programming enthusiast, just like many of you. My journey wasn't a smooth one. For a long time, I jumped between resources, solved problems randomly, got stuck repeatedly, and made far less progress than I expected.

Everything changed when I started approaching DSA in a structured way. Instead of chasing problem counts, I focused on understanding patterns, building intuition, and learning concepts in a logical order. The improvement that followed was faster than anything I had experienced before, and that experience ultimately inspired me to create Ascend.

Today, I'm still learning, still solving problems, and still trying to get better every day. Ascend is simply a reflection of everything I've learned throughout that journey - a collection of carefully selected problems organized in the way I wish I had discovered much earlier.

If you have questions, suggestions, feedback, or simply want to connect, feel free to reach out. I'd be happy to help and would love to hear from you.

After all, Ascend was built for learners, by a learner.
